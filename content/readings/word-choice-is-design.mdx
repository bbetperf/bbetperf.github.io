## The argument.

Every label, error message, and tooltip is a micro-decision that shapes how someone understands your product. Most designers treat copy as a final pass. It should be the first.

## Two examples.

Bad: "An error occurred. Please try again."
Better: "We couldn't save your changes — check your connection and retry."

The second costs nothing extra to write. It tells the user what happened and what to do. That is design.

## Practical rule.

Write the label before you design the component. If you can't name it clearly, you don't understand what it does yet.
